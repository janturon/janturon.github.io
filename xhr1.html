XHR

It is ridiculous to have a XMLHttpRequest "library". Native XMLHttpRequest is as simple as can be, any "enhancement" is just useless complication. Later, Promises were introduced in ES6: XHR just wraps XMLHttpRequest events into Promise executor.

<input type="number" id="number" value="1" autocomplete="off"> <button id="btn">Get number in English</button> <output id="out"></output> <script src="src/xhr.js"></script> <script> btn.onclick = _=> XHR('http://janturon.cz/number.php',{value: number.value}) .then(data => out.innerHTML = data); </script>
